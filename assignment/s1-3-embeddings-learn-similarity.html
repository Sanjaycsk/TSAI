What's happening?

In plain words

What's the claim?

Train a model only to predict the next token. You never tell it that Kohli and Maxwell are related — yet their embeddings end up side by side. Similarity appears for free.

What is an embedding?

The little list of numbers that stands in for a token (Rohan's "16 numbers for a word"). It starts as random noise; training slowly moves it to a meaningful spot.

Why do they cluster?

RCB players are all followed by the same next token ("RCB"). To predict that, the model is forced to give them near-identical embeddings. See the twin bars above — same prediction ⇒ same spot.

What was NOT told?

The word "team". No labels, no "these are similar". Only raw next-token pairs. The category is the output of learning, not the input — that's why we call it emergent.

Why "project to 2D"?

Real embeddings live in many dimensions (try dim 8 / 16). We can't see 8-D, so PCA squashes it to the 2 directions that spread the dots the most — just enough to eyeball the clumps.

The transfer twist

Hit Transfer Maxwell: now he appears in RCB and CSK sentences. His single embedding gets pulled between two clumps — exactly the "river-bank vs money-bank" problem from class.
