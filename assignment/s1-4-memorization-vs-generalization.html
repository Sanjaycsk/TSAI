What's happening?

In plain words

What's the trap?

A model that scores 100% on what it studied but flunks the real match. Perfect train accuracy can be a lie — it may have memorised, not learned.

Why does n=20 fail?

With more knobs than data, the net builds a lookup table — it loops its boundary around each dot, including the noisy flukes. Nothing transfers to new shots.

What is the gap?

test loss − train loss. A big gap = overfitting. Watch it: ~huge at n=20, then it shrinks toward zero as you slide the dataset bigger.

Why does data fix it?

You can't memorise 2000 shots, and the random flukes cancel out. The cheapest way to low loss becomes learning the true zone — which also works on unseen shots.

Note the floor

Test accuracy stops at ~85%, not 100% — because 15% of labels are noise. No model can beat pure randomness. That ceiling is the irreducible part.

The one takeaway

Data is everything. Same model, same capacity — the amount of data decided whether it memorised or understood. This is why frontier LLMs are obsessed with data.
