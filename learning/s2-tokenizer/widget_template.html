TSAI · ERA V5 · Session 2 — Tokenization

TokenSangam — can 4 languages share 10,000 tokens?

Sangam (संगम) = a confluence, where rivers meet — and that is literally this tokenizer: 9,744 merges flowing English, Hindi, Telugu and Kannada into one byte-level BPE vocabulary, built from scratch on the India Wikipedia page. The score is 1000 ÷ (max − min fertility), fertility = tokens per word. We built it twice: the first attempt hit a measured wall; the second changed one rule and broke through. Everything below is live — drag, type, paste a Wikipedia page.

Attempt 1 — word-BPE (the wall)
Merges stop at word edges and go to whichever language is furthest from its gate. English needs 6,364 merges for 1.19; getting hi/te/kn under 1.8 needs 11,019 total — only 9,744 exist. No split of the budget can fix arithmetic.
✗ at 10k: en 1.19 · indic 2.06 — score 1,151
Final — phrase-BPE (the breakthrough)
One rule change: merges may cross spaces (split only at sentence ends — the SentencePiece split_by_whitespace=false idea). One token can be " of the" or " के लिए" — English's bill drops to 4,390, and the savings carry every language under its gate. English freezes at 1.19 the moment it arrives (it's the score's minimum — helping it would widen the gap).
✓ at 10k: en 1.19 · hi/te/kn 1.73 — score 1,849

What we learned · what's next

The thinking that mattered, in four cards.

Was 10k too big or too small?

Both. Too small for word-BPE — the four languages' merge bills total 11,019, so the wall at indic ≈ 2.06 is arithmetic, not tuning. Yet the corpus runs out at 16,762 word-scope merges (every word already one token). Real models solve this with 100k–256k vocabs trained on terabytes, not one page.

The metric shapes the model

Score = 1000/(max−min) rewards uniformity. So the right moves are backwards-feeling: freeze the best language (improving the minimum widens the gap) and feed the worst. Past the peak, more learning makes the score worse. Always ask what a metric actually pays for.

Measure, then change the rules

We priced every language per target (merges needed) before believing any plan. 40/20/20/20 splits and multi-run token harvesting just re-slice a too-small pie — the breakthrough was growing the pie: let merges cross spaces, a legal tokenizer-design choice with a SentencePiece precedent.

What we'd build next

A unigram-LM tokenizer (pick tokens by likelihood, drop the greedy loop) · vocab pruning (train big, delete useless tokens) · fertility on unseen pages — phrase tokens memorize this page by design (the sanctioned cheat), so the paste-a-page box above is the honesty check.
